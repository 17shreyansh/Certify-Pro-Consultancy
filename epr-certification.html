EPR Certification

Extended Producer Responsibility Authorization for E-waste, Plastic Waste & Battery Waste Management

What is EPR Certification?

EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) is a crucial concept in waste management, especially for electronic products. The EPR certificate is mandatory for Indian manufacturers/importers of products falling under the E-waste category.

Obtaining EPR Authorization ensures that manufacturers and importers fulfill their legal obligations regarding responsible E-waste management. Under India's E-waste Management Rules, 2016, regulated by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC), producers of electrical and electronic equipment (EEE) are required to obtain EPR authorization.

Why EPR Certification is Important

Legal Compliance

Mandatory requirement under E-waste Management Rules, 2016 for manufacturers and importers.

Environmental Responsibility

Ensures proper collection, recycling, and disposal of electronic waste.

Market Access

Required for selling electronic products in the Indian market.

Avoid Penalties

Prevents legal penalties and non-compliance issues with regulatory authorities.

Brand Reputation

Demonstrates commitment to environmental sustainability and corporate responsibility.

Regulatory Oversight

Process overseen by Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) ensuring quality standards.
