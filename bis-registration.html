BIS Registration

Bureau of Indian Standards Certification for Quality Assurance

What is BIS Certification?

BIS certification refers to the process of certification by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), which is the national standards body of India. BIS is responsible for the development and certification of product standards to ensure quality, safety, and reliability of products sold in India.

BIS certification assures consumers that the products they purchase meet quality and safety standards, contributing to consumer protection and product reliability in the Indian market.

Types of BIS Certifications

Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS)

Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) has introduced the Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS) to ensure the quality and safety of products sold in India. Under this scheme, certain products are mandatory to get registered under BIS and comply with Indian safety standards before they can be legally sold in the Indian market.

ISI Mark Certification

The ISI mark, also known as the Indian Standard Institute mark, is a certification mark for industrial products in India. It signifies that a product conforms to the Indian Standards (IS) set by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), which is the national standards body of India. The presence of the ISI mark on a product assures consumers of its quality and safety, and it is often a requirement for manufacturers to sell their products in the Indian market.

Foreign Manufacturers Certification Scheme (FMCS)

Under the FMCS, foreign manufacturers are required to obtain BIS certification for their products. This involves testing the products in BIS recognized laboratories to ensure compliance with relevant Indian Standards. Once certified, the products can carry the BIS mark, indicating their conformity to Indian standards and making them eligible for import and sale in the Indian market.

Documents Required For Certifications

Documents Required for CRS Certification

  • Form I
  • Business license
  • ISO certification
  • Brand trademark
  • Brand Authorization letter
  • Brand declaration letter
  • Affidavit
  • Nomination letter
  • AIR ID Proof
  • AIR Authorization letter
  • AIR company GST
  • Test Report
  • Critical component list
  • Undertaking

Documents Required for ISI Certification

  • Business License / manufacturing License
  • MSME Certificate (If applicable)
  • Company PAN, GST/IEC and ISO
  • Director or Authorized Person KYC
  • Product Diagrams and Internal Drawings
  • In-house testing Facility
  • Details of manufacturing machinery
  • TradeMark Certificate
  • Quality Manual and QA Reports
  • Factory Layout
  • Process Flow chart
  • Factory Location with the nearest Landmark
  • List of test equipment with Calibration Certificate
  • Forms & Declaration Formats will be provided by us
  • SubContracting Agreement (If applicable)

Documents Required for FMCS Certification

  • Business License / manufacturing License (If in other languages, English translation is mandatory)
  • Process flow chart
  • If any, information about other certificates for products or factory systems
  • Company PAN, GST/IEC and ISO
  • Director or Authorized Person KYC
  • Product Diagrams and Internal Drawings
  • In-house testing Facility
  • List of manufacturing machinery
  • A list including information on test equipment and its Calibration Certificate
  • Information about a quality person
  • Test report according to Indian Standards
  • Declaration
  • Performance Bank Guarantee 10,000 USD
  • AIR (Authorized Indian Representative) Company and authorized person's details
