WPC Certification

Wireless Planning & Coordination Equipment Type Approval Services

What is WPC ETA Certification?

WPC (Wireless Planning & Coordination) ETA (Equipment Type Approval) certification is a crucial regulatory requirement enforced by the Government of India for all wireless products intended for sale and use within the Indian market. It ensures that wireless devices comply with the necessary technical standards and regulations set forth by the WPC, ensuring consumer safety, network compatibility, and efficient spectrum management.

Why is WPC ETA Certification Important?

Legal Compliance

WPC ETA certification is mandatory for wireless products to be legally sold and used in India. Non-compliance can result in severe penalties and market restrictions.

Quality Assurance

By undergoing the WPC ETA certification process, manufacturers guarantee that their wireless devices meet the required technical specifications and standards, ensuring quality and reliability for end-users.

Market Access

Obtaining WPC ETA certification grants access to the vast Indian market, allowing manufacturers to tap into new business opportunities and reach a broader consumer base.

Consumer Safety

Certified wireless products undergo rigorous testing to ensure they do not interfere with other wireless networks and operate safely within designated frequency bands, safeguarding consumer interests and safety.

Registration Process

1. Fill the Registration Form

The initial step is to complete the registration form, providing all the necessary details and information required by the WPC.

2. Product Testing

If the product successfully with stands the testing and complies with the prescribed standards, the WPC application will be accepted.

3. Application Acceptance

If the product successfully with stands the testing and complies with the prescribed standards, the WPC application will be accepted.

4. Submit Required Documents

Along with the filled registration form, applicants need to submit the required documents. These documents typically include technical specifications, test reports, and other relevant information about the wireless device.

5. Submit Registration Fee

Applicants are required to submit the registration fee along with the completed registration form and documents. The fee may vary depending on the type of wireless device and the services it offers.

6. Obtain WPC License

After the acceptance of the application, the WPC issues the necessary license for the wireless device. This license is essential for legally selling, producing, or importing the wireless equipment in the Indian market.
